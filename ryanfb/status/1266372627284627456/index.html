The penthemeros was an annual obligation in the Fayum to work on the dikes for five days (thus the name: πέντε "five" + ἡμέρας "days" = πενθήμερος). We have work certificates ranging from 45CE-218CE, highlighting its long importance for maintaining the system of irrigation.

Unlike some other forms of mandatory labor (see http://papyri.info/ddbdp/p.oxy;12;1409), the penthemeros obligation could be performed by someone on your behalf. In http://papyri.info/ddbdp/sb;18;13976, we have a unique witness of a free person performing the work on behalf of an enslaved man named Kerdon.
