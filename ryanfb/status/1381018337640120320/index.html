So what does "ΧΜΓϘΘ" mean? We don't really know. The "ΧΜΓ" part is frequently used in Christian contexts and on prayer amulets from around this time, though there are also variations, and other occurrences of it as "ΧΜΓϘΘ" as well.

Some argue for an acrostic interpretation, e.g. Χ(ριστὸς) Μ(αρίας) Γ(εννηθείς) (Christ born of Mary). Others argue for an isopsephistic interpretation, that is, the characters represent numbers that in turn represent another religious word or phrase.

In this case, ΧΜΓ = 600 (Χ) + 40 (Μ) + 3 (Γ) = 643, representing the phrase θεὸς βοηθός (Lord help us), which also sums to 643, while ϘΘ = 90 (Ϙ) + 9(Θ) = 99, similarly representing ἀμήν (amen)
