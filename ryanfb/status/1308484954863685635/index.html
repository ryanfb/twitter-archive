I'd only seen the "steep Wilusa" line on its own and out of context. But it's cooler than that! It's part of a whole Hittite tablet that lists the openings of several Luvian cult songs, alternating between languages. (See this post by @PeterGainsford: https://kiwihellenist.blogspot.com/2016/09/the-trojan-war-3-bronze-age-evidence.html)

(Hittite) Then he drinks to the god Suwasuna (and they sing:)
(Luvian) "When they came from steep Wilusa."

Note also that this isn't the only text or evidence we have for Wilusa—it's mentioned in other Hittite texts. CTH 182, "Letter from a king of Hatti (probably Tudhaliya IV) to a Western Anatolian Ruler", mentions sending wooden tablets and reinstalling one Walmu as king of Wilusa.
