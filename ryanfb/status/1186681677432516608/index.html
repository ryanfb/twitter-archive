Oh, Aelius Theon's got jokes (Progymnasmata 102.34-35):

Διδύμων ὁ αὐλητὴς ἁλοὺς ἐπὶ μοιχείᾳ ἐκ τοῦ ὀνόματος ἐκρεμάσθη.
Didymus the aulos-player, caught committing adultery, was hung by his own name.

("didymus" can mean "testicles", like calling them "the twins")

"Pythagoras the philosopher went off and became a grammar teacher, and used to advise his pupils to abstain from 'ἐναιμόνων'"

Where "ἐναιμόνων" can mean "blooded things", i.e. referring to Pythagorean vegetarianism, or instead referring to abstaining from the word 'ἐναιμόνων' itself - a form of the third-declension hapax legomenon 'ἐναίμων' vs. the more usual second-declension 'ἔναιμος'.
